Data Breach Due to Lack of Encryption

When we give our data to a company, we expect them to be good custodians and provide a basic level of protection. However, not all data is encrypted. While there are regulations for personal health data through HIPPA and laws governing credit card PCI DSS information (U.S. Federal Register at 74 FR 42740, August 24, 2009), other types of data may not be as tightly regulated. In fact, many enterprise data breaches have occurred simply due to a lack of data encryption.

Anthem's 2015 Crisis

One example of unencrypted data being breached happened in 2015, when Anthem's database was hacked (Whitney, 2015). The health insurance company breach affected a total of 80 million people, which included both employees and insurance customers (past and present). Even though credit card and medical data was protected by encryption, the Anthem breach involved people's other unregulated data, like member ID, names, social security numbers, date of birth, contact information, and employment information (Whitney, 2015).

Encryption is NOT Enough

A company risks their reputation with customers when they do not protect their customers data. Unfortunately, encryption is not a panacea; even when data is protected, ingenious hackers can still gain access to it. Social engineering and phishing schemes just scratch the surface of how hackers get unauthorized access to data.
