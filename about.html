About Me

I am redefining myself and following my passion for technology toward a career as a developer. To fully understand the path that I am now on, a view into where I have been is helpful. Please read on to learn more about who I am becoming based on what I have done in the past.

Researcher

After obtaining a Master's degree in Applied Psychology, I spent over a decade managing and performing experiments, collecting and analyzing data, and sharing my findings with the academic community. I worked on a variety of projects aimed at rehabilitating patients with vision deficits as well as basic research on how the human visual system works.

Adjunct Professor

Docendo discimus is latin for "by teaching, we learn" and is attributed to Seneca the Younger, (circa 4 BC — 65 AD). I found this proverb to be true when I worked as an adjunct professor for 5 years. I developed curriculum, lectured, assessed student learning; but, most of all I gained a deeper understanding and expertise in the topics I taught.

Homeschool Instructor

Had it not been for teaching at the college level, I would probably not have trusted my teaching skills enough to become a homeschool mom. I was concerned about not being able to provide the same level of instruction that a middle school child would be exposed to at a traditional school with a vast number subject matter experts and resources. Hindsight being 20/20, I had nothing to fear: I found I had a natural aptitude for explaining complex concepts in plain terms and a gift for igniting the fire to learn.

Private Tutor

Starting my private tutoring business has been a way for me to pass forward the knowledge I have acquired over the years. I get a particular satisfaction from bringing a student who struggles in some area to a level of mastery where they are comfortable with the material and excited to learn more.

Becoming a Student Again

Everyone who I have taught to date has heard me at one time or other explain that learning has less to do with natural aptitude and more to do with the willingness to spend the time necessary to aquire new knowledge and skills. It isn't enough to believe in what you say, but to live it. This is precisely why I have gone back to school to learn Computer Science. I know that given enough time, I will become a subject matter expert in whatever academic silo I aim my career at next.
